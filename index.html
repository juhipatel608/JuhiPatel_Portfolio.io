This is my Story

I was first introduced to coding when I learned Java in my high school AP Computer Science class. I was amazed at how I could work with the computer to create a space invaders game from scratch. It reminded me of dissecting a cat in Biology but get to see the inner workings of a program. I then went on to get my Bachelor of Science of Sociology at Texas A&M University. During my time there one of my favorite classes was the Sociology of Science and Technology and I grew a strong interest how society interacts with technology. We discussed many topics like genetic modification, machine learning , and AI. While it captivating to talk about these concepts, I wanted to really be a part of it.

When I graduated, I took a job in insurance but after two and half years I felt stuck. I craved to be challenged wanted to grow everyday. So I decided I had to finally do something I was passionate about. I did some research and found out about the University of Texas coding bootcamp, which seemed like the perfect opportunity for me. So I took the plunge and it became clear very quickly that I made the right choice. I found the drive to learn again. I thought to myself "why didn't I just do this from the start". But I realized my history in sociology taught me to understand the big picture of what software development was about. I also now knew that I never wanted to be in a job that didnt truly excite and engage me. I am grateful for the journey that brought me to where I am.

Where I am now:

Full-stack developer currently seeking to advance career in software development. Passionate about approaching programming challenges from different angles, and collaborating with others to create meaningful products. Enjoys leveraging background in Sociology, Customer Service and a life-long dedication to learning to provide unique perspectives on how users interact with software platforms and implementing these skills into the Agile environment.
